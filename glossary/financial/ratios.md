# Financial Ratios

Key financial ratios used for company analysis and comparison.

## Profitability Ratios

### Gross Margin
$$\text{Gross Margin} = \frac{\text{Revenue} - \text{COGS}}{\text{Revenue}}$$
- Measures production efficiency
- Higher is generally better
- Varies by industry

### Operating Margin  
$$\text{Operating Margin} = \frac{\text{Operating Income}}{\text{Revenue}}$$
- Core business profitability
- Excludes interest and taxes
- Good for operational efficiency comparison

### Net Margin
$$\text{Net Margin} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Revenue}}$$
- Overall profitability
- Bottom-line measure
- Includes all expenses

### Return on Assets (ROA)
$$\text{ROA} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Total Assets}}$$
- Efficiency of asset use
- Higher is better
- Industry-dependent

### Return on Equity (ROE)
$$\text{ROE} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Shareholder Equity}}$$
- Return to shareholders
- Important for equity investors
- Can be inflated by leverage

## Liquidity Ratios

### Current Ratio
$$\text{Current Ratio} = \frac{\text{Current Assets}}{\text{Current Liabilities}}$$
- Short-term liquidity
- >1 indicates ability to pay short-term obligations
- Too high may indicate inefficient cash use

### Quick Ratio
$$\text{Quick Ratio} = \frac{\text{Current Assets} - \text{Inventory}}{\text{Current Liabilities}}$$
- Acid test ratio
- Excludes less liquid inventory
- More conservative than current ratio

## Solvency Ratios

### Debt-to-Equity
$$\text{D/E} = \frac{\text{Total Debt}}{\text{Shareholder Equity}}$$
- Financial leverage
- Higher = more risk
- Varies significantly by industry

### Interest Coverage
$$\text{Interest Coverage} = \frac{\text{EBIT}}{\text{Interest Expense}}$$
- Ability to pay interest
- Higher is better
- <1.5 may indicate distress

## Valuation Ratios

### Price-to-Earnings (P/E)
$$\text{P/E} = \frac{\text{Price per Share}}{\text{EPS}}$$
- Market expectations
- Compare to peers and historical
- Can be forward or trailing

### Price-to-Book (P/B)
$$\text{P/B} = \frac{\text{Market Cap}}{\text{Book Value}}$$
- Market vs accounting value
- <1 may indicate undervaluation
- Less relevant for intangible-heavy businesses

### Enterprise Value to EBITDA
$$\text{EV/EBITDA} = \frac{\text{Enterprise Value}}{\text{EBITDA}}$$
- Capital structure neutral
- Good for M&A comparison
- Widely used in private equity

## Efficiency Ratios

### Asset Turnover
$$\text{Asset Turnover} = \frac{\text{Revenue}}{\text{Total Assets}}$$
- Efficiency of asset use
- Higher is better
- Industry-dependent

### Inventory Turnover
$$\text{Inventory Turnover} = \frac{\text{COGS}}{\text{Average Inventory}}$$
- Inventory management efficiency
- Higher is generally better
- Too high may indicate stockouts
