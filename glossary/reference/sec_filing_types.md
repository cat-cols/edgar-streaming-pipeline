# SEC Filing Types

Common SEC filing types and their purposes in financial analysis.

## Periodic Reports

### 10-K (Annual Report)
- Comprehensive annual report
- Filed within 60-90 days after fiscal year-end
- **Contents:**
  - Business overview
  - Risk factors
  - Financial statements (audited)
  - Management discussion & analysis (MD&A)
  - Market data
  - Legal proceedings

### 10-Q (Quarterly Report)
- Quarterly financial report
- Filed within 40-45 days after quarter-end
- **Contents:**
  - Unaudited financial statements
  - MD&A
  - Legal proceedings
  - Changes in securities

### 8-K (Current Report)
- Report of material events
- Filed within 4 business days of event
- **Triggering Events:**
  - Entry into material agreement
  - Completion of acquisition/disposition
  - Results of operations and financial condition
  - Creation of direct financial obligation
  - Events related to accountants
  - Change in securities
  - Change in registrant's certifying accountant
  - Non-reliance on previously issued financial statements
  - Change in fiscal year

## Registration Statements

### S-1 (Registration Statement)
- Initial registration of securities
- Used for IPOs
- **Contents:**
  - Business description
  - Use of proceeds
  - Dilution
  - Capitalization
  - Management
  - Financial statements

### S-3 (Registration Statement)
- Simplified registration for seasoned issuers
- Faster shelf registration process
- For companies meeting certain requirements

## Proxy Statements

### DEF 14A (Proxy Statement)
- Information for shareholder meetings
- **Contents:**
  - Board of directors information
  - Executive compensation
  - Related party transactions
  - Proposal descriptions
  - Voting procedures

## Ownership Reports

### 13F (Institutional Manager Holdings Report)
- Quarterly report of institutional holdings
- Filed by investment managers with $100M+ AUM
- **Contents:**
  - List of securities held
  - Number of shares
  - Value of holdings

### 13D (Beneficial Ownership Report)
- Report of 5%+ ownership
- Filed within 10 days of reaching threshold
- **Contents:**
  - Identity of filing person
  - Purpose of transaction
  - Source of funds
  - Future plans

### 13G (Beneficial Ownership Report)
- Passive investment 5%+ ownership
- Similar to 13D but for passive investors
- Filed within 45 days

## Tender Offers

### SC 13E3 (Going Private Transaction)
- Information about going-private transactions
- Filed by companies going private
- **Contents:**
  - Terms of transaction
  - Fairness opinion
  - Background of transaction

## Other Common Filings

### 20-F (Foreign Private Issuer)
- Annual report for foreign companies
- Similar to 10-K but with some differences
- Filed by foreign companies listed in US

### 6-K (Report of Foreign Private Issuer)
- Information furnished to foreign regulators
- Equivalent of 8-K for foreign companies

### 4 (Statement of Changes in Beneficial Ownership)
- Reports insider trading
- Filed by officers, directors, 10% shareholders
- **Contents:**
  - Transaction details
  - Price and quantity
  - Remaining ownership

### 144 (Notice of Proposed Sale of Securities)
- Intent to sell restricted securities
- Required before selling restricted stock

### 11-K (Annual Report of Employee Stock Purchase Plans)
- Information about ESPPs
- Filed with 10-K
